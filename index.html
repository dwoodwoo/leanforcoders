About Lean for Coders

2016-05-03

Among software and application developers, particularly those who participate in a lot of hackathons, there’s a culture of code-first (or create product-first) and then find an audience or a market.

Much time and effort is spent trying to build features to increase an app’s audience. If those features don’t lift growth or find an audience, oftentimes, the remedy is to build more code. Pretty soon, the app looks like spaghetti, there are too many features (making UI a nightmare – how do you prioritize one feature versus another on your limited screen real estate?).

And on top of that, the audience still hasn’t increased.

The Lean Startup movement addresses this:

The main reason a startup (or an app) fails is not because it’s technically deficient.
The main reason for failure is that the startup has built a product that nobody wants.

As it turns out, building features is one of the more expensive ways to validate your business idea. What has emerged over the last few years with the Lean Startup movement is a revolution in approaching business validation much the way that Agile methodologies revolutionized product management over a decade ago.

This blog is an effort to introduce software developers who have be primarily focused on code to Lean Startup practices and practices such as Customer Development and Inbound Marketing.

Adopting and gaining discipline in these techniques may be the make or break difference in your startup.
